## Understanding Cancer Care Needs

### Types of Cancer Care

| Stage | Care Focus | Setting |
|-------|------------|---------|
| **During Treatment** | Recovery between chemo/radiation | Hospital or skilled nursing |

- Pain management
- Medication administration
- Wound care (post-surgery)
- Nutritional support
- Emotional support
- Symptom management
- Mobility assistance
- End-of-life care (if terminal)

- Emotional and spiritual support
- Family support

**Best For**: Terminal diagnosis (typically 6 months or less)

**Services**:
- Comfort-focused care
- Pain management
- Emotional support
- Family bereavement support
- Peaceful end-of-life care

## Palliative Care - Understanding It

### What Palliative Care IS:

✅ Comfort and quality of life focus
✅ Pain and symptom management
✅ Emotional and spiritual support
✅ Can be provided alongside treatment
✅ For any serious illness stage
✅ Family support included

### What Palliative Care IS NOT:

❌ "Giving up"
❌ Only for dying patients
❌ Stopping all treatment
❌ The same as hospice
❌ Speeding up death

**Important**: Palliative care can help at ANY stage of cancer, even while actively treating.

## Hospice Care - When Comfort is the Goal

### When to Consider Hospice

- Prognosis of 6 months or less
- Decision to focus on comfort vs. cure
- Treatment is no longer effective
- Patient/family choose quality over quantity

### What Hospice Provides

- Round-the-clock symptom management
- Emotional and spiritual support
- Family support and education
- Bereavement services
- Peaceful, dignified end-of-life care

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Can nursing homes care for cancer patients?

Yes, many nursing homes can provide care for cancer patients, especially for supportive care, recovery, and palliative needs. Complex treatment is done in hospitals.

### What's the difference between palliative care and hospice?

Palliative care is comfort-focused care at any illness stage, alongside treatment. Hospice is for terminal patients (typically 6 months prognosis) focusing entirely on comfort.

### How much does hospice care cost in Malaysia?

Hospice care ranges from RM3,000-7,000/month in facilities. Many non-profit hospices offer subsidized or free services based on financial need.

### Can cancer patients receive chemotherapy while in nursing home?

Chemotherapy is administered at hospitals/oncology centers. Nursing homes provide supportive care between treatments and help with recovery.

### Is hospice care "giving up"?

No. Hospice is choosing quality of life and comfort when cure is no longer possible. It's a positive choice for dignity and peace.

### How do I know when it's time for hospice?

Discuss with the oncologist when treatment options are exhausted or when the focus shifts from cure to comfort. It's a personal and family decision.
