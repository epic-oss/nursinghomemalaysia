# Nursing Homes for Bedridden Patients in Malaysia {year}

## What is Bedridden Care?

Bedridden care is for patients who:

- Cannot get out of bed independently
- Require full assistance for all daily activities
- Need regular repositioning to prevent bed sores
- May require medical equipment (feeding tubes, catheters)
- Need 24-hour monitoring and nursing care

### Common Conditions Leading to Bedridden Status

- Severe stroke
- Advanced Parkinson's disease
- Late-stage dementia
- Spinal cord injuries
- Severe arthritis
- Post-major surgery recovery
- Terminal illness
- Severe weakness from chronic disease

## Bed Sore Prevention - Critical Factor

Bed sores (pressure ulcers) are a major concern for bedridden patients. Good facilities should have:

### Prevention Measures

| Measure | Standard |
|---------|----------|
| Repositioning | Every 2-3 hours |
| Mattress Type | Air or pressure-relieving |
| Skin Inspection | Daily |
| Moisture Control | Keep skin dry |
| Nutrition | Adequate protein intake |

## Frequently Asked Questions

### How much does bedridden care cost in Malaysia?

Bedridden care costs RM3,000-10,000/month depending on location and care complexity. It's typically 30-50% more than standard nursing home care.

### Can nursing homes handle feeding tubes?

Many can, but not all. Specifically ask about enteral feeding (tube feeding) capability when enquiring.

### How often should bedridden patients be repositioned?

Every 2-3 hours is the medical standard to prevent bed sores. Good facilities follow this strictly.

### Is 24-hour nursing care available?

Yes, facilities specializing in bedridden care have nurses available round-the-clock, not just caregivers.

### What equipment should a good facility have?

Hospital beds, air/pressure-relieving mattresses, patient lifts, and emergency equipment are essential for bedridden care.

### Can I still provide some care during visits?

Yes, most facilities welcome family involvement in non-medical care like feeding, grooming, and companionship.
