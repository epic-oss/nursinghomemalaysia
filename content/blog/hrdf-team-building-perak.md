## Understanding HRDF Claims for Adventure Activities

### Making Adventure Claimable

Adventure activities can qualify for HRDF when structured properly:

**Claimable structure:**
- Clear learning objectives tied to activities
- Pre-activity briefing on teamwork elements
- Facilitated debrief after each activity
- Connection to workplace application
- Documented outcomes

**Example: White Water Rafting**

*Non-claimable approach:*
- "Fun rafting trip for the team"

*Claimable approach:*
- Leadership rotation during rapids
- Communication protocol practice
- Risk assessment exercise
- Post-activity debrief on coordination
- Documented learning outcomes

### Learning Outcomes from Adventure

**Activities teach:**

| Activity | Learning Outcomes |
|----------|-------------------|
| Rafting | Team coordination, communication under pressure |
| Caving | Trust, following instructions, overcoming fears |
| High elements | Risk management, encouraging others |
| Jungle trek | Pacing, supporting team members, persistence |

## Claim Process Specifics

### Documentation for Adventure Programmes

**Standard requirements:**
- Attendance records
- Programme outline
- Learning outcomes
- Assessment results
- Trainer credentials

**Adventure-specific additions:**
- Safety briefing records
- Activity completion records
- Debrief documentation
- Learning connection worksheets

### Ensuring Claim Success

**Structure is key:**
1. Clear learning objectives from start
2. Briefing before each activity
3. Facilitated debrief after activities
4. Written reflection by participants
5. Assessment of learning outcomes

**Common claim issues:**
- Activities not linked to learning
- Missing pre/post assessments
- Inadequate documentation
- Non-certified trainers
