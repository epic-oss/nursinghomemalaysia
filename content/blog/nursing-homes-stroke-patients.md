## Understanding Stroke Care Needs

### Types of Stroke Effects

Stroke patients may experience:

| Effect | Care Needed |
|--------|-------------|
| **Paralysis (one side)** | Mobility assistance, physiotherapy |
| **Speech difficulties** | Speech therapy |
| **Swallowing problems** | Modified diet, feeding assistance |
| **Cognitive changes** | Mental stimulation, supervision |
| **Incontinence** | Personal care assistance |
| **Emotional changes** | Emotional support, monitoring |

### Stroke Recovery Stages

**Acute Phase (0-3 months)**
- Hospital-based care
- Intensive rehabilitation
- Medical stabilization

**Sub-acute Phase (3-6 months)**
- Nursing home or rehab center
- Continued therapy
- Learning to adapt

**Chronic Phase (6+ months)**
- Long-term care if needed
- Maintenance therapy
- Quality of life focus

## Levels of Stroke Care

### Level 1: Mild Stroke

- Some weakness but mobile
- Can do some self-care
- Needs supervision and therapy
- **Care Focus**: Rehabilitation, independence
- **Cost**: Lower end

### Level 2: Moderate Stroke

- Significant one-sided weakness
- Needs help with most activities
- Can participate in therapy
- **Care Focus**: Intensive rehab, assistance
- **Cost**: Mid range

### Level 3: Severe Stroke

- Little or no movement
- Bedridden or wheelchair-bound
- May have swallowing/communication issues
- **Care Focus**: Full care, quality of life
- **Cost**: Higher end

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Can stroke patients fully recover in nursing homes?

Recovery depends on stroke severity and individual factors. Many patients improve significantly with proper therapy, though some effects may be permanent.

### How often should stroke patients have physiotherapy?

Ideally 3-5 times per week in early recovery. Frequency may decrease to 1-2 times weekly for maintenance in later stages.

### Do all nursing homes offer stroke rehabilitation?

No. Many provide basic care but not active rehabilitation. Ask specifically about therapy services when enquiring.

### Is it better to recover at home or in a nursing home?

Nursing homes often provide more consistent therapy and 24-hour care. Home recovery works well with strong family support and outpatient therapy access.

### How long do stroke patients stay in nursing homes?

Varies widely - from a few months for rehabilitation to permanent residence for those with lasting disabilities. Care should be reassessed regularly.

### Can speech recover after stroke?

Many patients see speech improvement with therapy, especially in the first year. Recovery varies based on stroke location and severity.
