## Understanding HRDF/HRD Corp Claims

### What Is HRDF?

Human Resources Development Fund (now HRD Corp) is a levy system where registered employers contribute 1% of employee salaries. These funds can be claimed back for approved training programmes, including structured team building.

### Key Requirements for Claims

**Employer requirements:**
- Registered with HRD Corp
- Levy payments up to date
- Sufficient balance in account

**Programme requirements:**
- Conducted by registered training provider
- Has defined learning outcomes
- Includes structured curriculum
- Proper documentation provided

**Participant requirements:**
- Malaysian citizens or permanent residents
- Employed by the claiming company
- Attendance properly documented

## Why Team Building Qualifies for HRDF

Not all team building is claimable. The key difference:

**Claimable:** Structured programmes with learning outcomes
- Communication skills development
- Leadership training through activities
- Problem-solving workshops
- Team effectiveness programmes

**Not claimable:** Pure recreation
- Fun days without learning structure
- Entertainment events
- Parties or celebrations

The same activities (obstacle courses, challenges) can be claimable when properly structured with learning objectives and professional facilitation.

## Claim Process for Johor Programmes

### Before the Programme

1. **Verify provider registration** on HRD Corp portal
2. **Check levy balance** sufficient for claim
3. **Submit grant application** (if required by scheme)
4. **Confirm participant eligibility**

### During the Programme

1. **Collect attendance** (sign in/out)
2. **Complete assessments** (pre and post)
3. **Participate fully** (minimum attendance required)
4. **Document activities** (photos with consent)

### After the Programme

1. **Receive documentation package** from provider
2. **Submit claim** within timeframe
3. **Provide supporting documents**
4. **Track claim status**

### Required Documentation

**From provider:**
- Training completion report
- Attendance records
- Assessment results
- Trainer credentials
- Programme outline
- Invoice and receipts

**From employer:**
- Claim application form
- Participant list
- Payment proof
- Company authorisation

## Johor-Specific Considerations

### Singapore Companies

Many Singapore companies bring teams to Johor for:
- Cost savings vs Singapore venues
- Change of environment
- Larger venue options
- Malaysian provider rates

**Note:** HRDF is for Malaysian employers. Singapore companies cannot claim but benefit from competitive pricing.

### Cross-Border Logistics

**For JB programmes:**
- Easy access via Causeway
- Consider traffic timing
- Multiple entry points available

**For Desaru:**
- 1.5-2 hours from JB
- Direct from Singapore via ferry (seasonal)
- Private transport recommended

### Weather Considerations

**Best months:** February-October
**Monsoon:** November-January (affects Desaru more)
**Plan B:** Indoor options always recommended
